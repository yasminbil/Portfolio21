Gen Z x Environment

A case study for ClimateView, a startup who helps cities to collate climate information so that they can understand what challenges and solutions there are. The goal was to give ClimateView insights from our research on what high school students want in terms of being engaged and well-read about the climate challenges we face.

Research, Analysis & Strategy

The Problem

Many people know that human beings affect the climate and great initiatives are often started to make things better. ClimateView wants to help through accelerating these initiatives. ‍ Our assignment was to answer this problem statement: “How can we design a service that generates local engagement amongst young people?”

More Context

This project was part of the course UX Process 1: Analysis and Strategy, at Nackademin’s UX Design Programme. It was a group project consisting of 5 members.

My Role

Finding the target group, conducting interviews, doing a thematic analysis, market intelligence mapping and quantitative research (surveys), developing a persona and user journey, Impact Mapping.

Target Group

High School Students (Gymnasium), ages 16-19.

Prototyping Tool

Figma.

Time Frame

7 weeks.

The Process

Discover & Define

Stakeholder mapping and interviews
We made a stakeholder map of people we were interested to hear from. We analysed how easy these stakeholders were to get hold of and also how much knowledge they could give us. We interviewed not only high school students but also elementary school pupils to make sure we didn’t miss out on relevant knowledge. We made the decision to focus on high school students as they make more independent decisions. We also included teachers and a journalist to get their perspectives. We transcribed the interviews and highlighted words, sentences and paragraphs we wanted to further investigate in a thematic analysis.
Thematic Analysis
We coded the information from the interviews and sorted this into different themes. This led us to quite a few insights about the target group.

Insights

Wants & Needs
  • Want to be able to do something in everyday situations, and get confirmation that they’ve done something right.
  • Want it to be easy to make sustainable choices.
  • Want simple and accessible information through familiar channels on how to act.
  • Want to feel togetherness and be boosted by what others do.
  • Want to see a positive change.
Frustrations
  • Too much advice makes it hard to navigate.
  • Don’t want to sacrifice comfort if they don’t know it actually helps the climate.
  • Prioritising friends before working against climate change.
  • Engaging in climate aspects seems too big.
  • Media’s negative side is what sticks and contributes to a feeling of hopelessness.

Persona

The insights were incorporated into the needs and frustrations of our persona Anna Eriksson.

User Journey

We wanted to put Anna in a situation to visualize where her pain points were.

Survey

We used a survey to get more insight into areas we hadn’t covered in our qualitative research, and to confirm some of the insights from the interviews.

Our Conclusion: Use the Teachers

During our interviews we noticed that schools are an important channel for climate information. The curriculum states that environmental aspects should be part of school but it doesn’t state how. The teachers can reach many students but may need more relevant material as a help. ‍ One solution to engaging young people could be to make the data and material accessible and useful to teachers. Below we've made a proto-persona of a teacher. To become a persona to base decisions on it would need more research.

Reflections

Group Dynamics
With such a big and complex problem it was hard to navigate and keep motivated at times. Our group introduced activities such as check ins/outs, to understand each other better and make sure we are working towards the same goal.
Confirm insights instead of broadening knowledge
It seemed like our questions never ended, we just wanted to know more. When sending out the survey we therefore broadened our knowledge about the target group even more, but in hindsight I feel like we should’ve focused more on confirming our qualitative research with the surveys instead.
Having more regular contact with client
It would’ve been great if we had booked in frequent meetings with our client to show our progress and see if we were on the right track according to them.
