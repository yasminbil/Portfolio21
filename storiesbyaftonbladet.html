Stories by Aftonbladet

Aftonbladet, a popular magazine in Sweden, wanted our help to figure out what the younger generation prefers in terms of consuming news.

UX/UI

The Problem

How do you get the younger generation, who is already fed with lots of information, to notice your product? How do you get them to read the news on your app when you compete with so many other things that are on their minds?

More Context

This project was part of a UX introduction course at Nackademin’s UX Design Programme. It was a group project consisting of 4 members.

My Role

Analyzing interviews, doing a competitive analysis, brainstorming together with the group, visual design in Figma.

Target Group

Generation Z, more specifically ages 17 to 23.

Prototyping Tool

Figma.

Time Frame

1 week.

The Process

Discover

Interviews and Competitive Analysis
We started off by getting to know our target audience through interviews while also conducting a competitive analysis of popular apps among Gen Z and other news apps.

Define

Insights from the interviews
Thematic Analysis
After coding the interviews and sorting wordings into appropriate themes with post-its, we got an understanding of the needs and the frustrations of Gen Z when consuming news today.
Wants & Needs
  • To be engaged in environmental, political and societal matters
  • To be able to discuss with friends
Frustrations
  • The articles are too long
  • The language is hard
  • The feed is too messy
Defining the Problem
Persona
Following the thematic analysis, a persona with the same needs and frustrations as previously mentioned was discussed and given a name, age and a face.
User Story
We discussed a User Story for the persona and ended up with: ‍ “As a Gen Z in my early 20’s, I want to, despite the fact that I’m not that informed on a specific topic, understand current matters.”
How might we
Based on the User Story this HMW was constructed: ‍ "How Might We inspire Gen Z to acquaint themselves with news they have little to no prior knowledge about?ormed on a specific topic, understand current matters.”

Deliver

Design development
After the brainstorming session we made some sketches and wireframes to ensure we were all on the same page. With our insights from the interviews and the competitive analysis, we noticed Gen Z turns to Instagram and TikTok a lot. We wanted to explore using a format that they are already used to, to encourage confident usage.
The Concept
Our idea was to let the user scroll in a feed just like Instagram, and be able to click on an image/article to know more. This would take the user to a full screen with visuals and simple text about the matter, for them to learn more - a bit like the Stories function on Instagram.
Answering to their Frustrations
Remember the frustrations Gen Z had? Messy feed, long articles with difficult language... Let's check out how we answered to them:
  • Using full screen images/videos to keep it simple and avoid distractions.
  • Storytelling properties where users can tap themselves further into the article.
  • The article itself has visuals with simple and brief information, to keep the users’ attention.
Moodboard
Based on our insights we thought it was a good idea to deviate from the classic Aftonbladet style to create a totally different feel of the app for users who currently don’t prefer the current design of Aftonbladet’s app. ‍ Our inspiration was the show Euphoria, which has a storyline about Gen Z. We wanted to use purple which stands for creativity and blue for stability.

Reflections

The Tight Time Frame
This was an intense week where I learned a lot and got to familiarise myself with the UX process. If there had been more time I would’ve wanted to dig deeper into every tool we used and explored even more tools like user journeys and a value proposition canvas.
Testing the Design
Unfortunately there was no time for testing the design on potential users. This would’ve given us valuable feedback to be able to go back to the drawing table and reiterate the design.
Incorporating Advertisement
There’s still an importance for Aftonbladet to run ads as this is partly how they are financed. To be able to show them that we had thought of the business side of things by incorporating a slide with an ad into the home page or during the full screen story would’ve been a nice touch.
