Workshop - Idea Development

In the course UX Process 1, we held a workshop for our fellow classmates who had already ideated on solutions to their problem in a previous workshop. We wanted them to now choose and develop an idea that would solve a problem they had defined.

The Problem

With a complex problem statement it can be challenging for a group to choose the right solution unanimously. This can be facilitated by bringing the group together to brainstorm.

More Context

This workshop was part of the course UX Process 1: Analysis and Strategy at Nackademin’s UX Design Programme. It was a chance to work on our workshop facilitation skills on our fellow classmates. The workshop was held online due to Covid-19.

My Role

Making and designing the workshop structure. Facilitating a group of 5 students.

Programme used

Miro.

Time Frame

2 hours workshop time.

The Workshop Playfield

By gathering information about the team we were to assist, and where they got to in their last workshop, we were able to set up the workshop structure in advance.

The Parking Lot

Just before we got into the actual workshop, we introduced the parking lot for all the ideas the team would come up with that didn’t necessarily fit into to the scope of this workshop.

Intro and Check In

An intro was done to make sure the team knew the agenda and the rules for the workshop (like turning the camera on etc). We used the check in to be able to understand where the team felt like they were at, getting a bit of insight into their status. They were asked to place a wagon on a rollercoaster to visualize their feelings.

Recap from Previous Workshop

We spent a few minutes walking the team through a recap of what the result was from their previous workshop, making sure they’re happy to continue with the ideas that had the most votes.

Choosing the Winner Idea

To validate which idea to move on with we let the team place the ideas on an impact/effort diagram and choose one winner idea (ideally an idea that’s been placed in the above left corner).

Start Developing the Idea

We wanted the team to brainstorm on solutions for the idea. This was done in two iterations where they dot voted on the one they wanted to dig deeper with.

Summary, Next Steps and Check Out

Once the team had developed their idea, we summarized what they had come up with and suggested next steps that they could do as a team. If there’s a check in there’s a check out! We wanted the team to reflect on if they felt differently to what they felt at the beginning of the workshop.

Getting Feedback Right Away

Before letting the group go, we let them write down some feedback they had for us.

Reflections

Online vs Physical Workshop
It worked pretty well doing the workshop in Miro. We tried to figure out a smooth way to get the team to draw their ideas, either in a drawing programme or on physical paper instead of writing it down, but we figured the logistics for that could be time consuming. In hindsight we could’ve taken the risk; we did end up finishing earlier than anticipated anyway.
Anchoring Decisions to the Persona
One set of feedback we got was about their persona; how the team didn’t feel like they had based their ideas on their persona’s wants and needs. In the future I would like to ask for a copy of the persona and paste it into the workshop field and continuously remind them about it throughout the workshop.
